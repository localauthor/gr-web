“when we do not understand something, it is usually because either it has no structure, or we cannot figure out the structure.” (Zagzebski 126)

“The object of understanding can be anything that has structure: a living organism, an event, a narrative, a piece of music, a work of art, a metaphysical system, a philosophical argument, a causal relation, the stock market, human intentional action, a moral theory.” (Zagzebski 124)

“knowledge can be isolated or episodic in ways that understanding cannot be. Thus one can know individual or isolated facts about a subject matter, but understanding seems to come in larger packages. Understanding ‘hangs together’ in ways that knowledge need not.” (Greco 288)
